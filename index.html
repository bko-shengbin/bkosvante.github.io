About Svante

Svante General Information Description.

It was founded in 2007, and its headquarters is in Burnaby, British Columbia. Developer of an adsorption technology designed to lower the cost curve of greenhouse gas emissions from post-combustion industrial and power plant flue gas. The company's technology captures post-combustion carbon dioxide from industrial fuel gas streams through the use of a rotary adsorption machine which functions as a rotary air preheater maximizing carbon capture productivity, enabling enterprises to cut capital costs and reducing energy regeneration.

What does Svante do?

About Svante

Svante offers companies in emission-intensive industries a commercially viable way to capture large-scale CO₂ emissions from existing infrastructure, either for safe storage or to be used for further industrial use in a closed loop. Its purpose is to create world-changing solutions that address climate change and accelerate the global transition to carbon neutrality, reversing human impact on the climate.

Carbon capture technology

Svante develops technology that captures carbon dioxide from flue gas.

Solid sorbents, particularly metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), are a step change for the carbon capture industry. Svante uses MOF to capture CO₂ from diluted flue gas streams with high capacity and selectivity over water. It captures 95% of the total CO₂ emitted from industrial sources, adsorbing CO₂ using direct low-pressure steam injection for regeneration. Concentrates it, then releases it for safe storage or industrial use, all in 60 seconds. The company's approach tailors specifically to the challenges of separating carbon dioxide from nitrogen contained in diluted flue gas generated by industrial plants such as cement, steel, aluminum, fertilizer, and hydrogen, which is typically emitted in large volumes, at low pressures, and dilute concentrations. Svante’s carbon capture technology consists of a patented architecture of structured adsorbent laminate (spaced sheets), proprietary process cycle design, and a rotary mechanical contactor to capture, release and regenerate the adsorbent in a single unit. Based on environmentally friendly solid sorbent technology, Svante carbon capture and removal solutions offer companies in heavy emitting, hard-to-decarbonize industries a commercially viable way to capture and remove CO₂ emissions. The CO₂ Svante capture is concentrated to pipeline grade purity, which can be safely transported and stored underground or used to make other products.

ICS3 International Carbon Capture Center

Svante, in partnership with Electricore Inc., creates ICS3 to focus on the demonstration, testing, and education of second-generation CO₂ capture technology using advanced solid adsorbent materials.

The center’s objective is to move novel solid adsorbent materials from the laboratory to operational power and industrial plants, and to establish new standards in the characterization of new adsorbent materials and benchmark the performance of CO₂ capture processes using rapid cycles.
